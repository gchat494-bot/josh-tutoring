Solving Systems with Desmos

The digital SAT includes a built-in Desmos graphing calculator. Knowing how to use it for systems of equations can save you significant time on test day — especially for checking answers or bypassing messy algebra.

What Is Desmos on the SAT?

The digital SAT (Bluebook) has a built-in Desmos graphing calculator available on the calculator-permitted section. You can access it by clicking the calculator icon in the top right during the test. It's the same Desmos you can practice with at desmos.com/calculator.

How to Solve a System in Desmos
1
Open Desmos during the test (calculator icon, top right of screen).
2
Type the first equation exactly as written in the problem. Desmos accepts any form — y = 2x + 1 or 3x + 2y = 12 both work.
3
Press Enter and type the second equation in the next line.
4
Click the intersection point where the two lines cross. Desmos will display the exact coordinates — that's your (x, y) solution!
5
If the lines don't intersect (parallel), Desmos confirms no solution. If they overlap, it's infinitely many solutions.

When to Use Desmos vs. Algebra
SituationUse Desmos?Why
Problem asks for exact (x, y) valuesYes ✓Click the intersection point for instant answer
Messy fractions or decimals in equationsYes ✓Desmos handles these without arithmetic errors
You want to check your algebraic answerYes ✓Takes 20 seconds to verify
Problem asks "how many solutions?" with parameters (like find k)No ✗Algebra required — Desmos can't solve for unknown constants
Problem asks for a combination like x + yNo ✗Use the SAT shortcut — much faster
Problem involves abstract/symbolic coefficientsNo ✗Desmos needs actual numbers to graph

Don't rely on Desmos for everything. It won't help with conceptual questions about the number of solutions or problems with unknown parameters like k. Make sure you understand the algebra too — Desmos is a tool, not a replacement for understanding.

Practice tip: Go to desmos.com/calculator right now and type in a system of two equations. Click the intersection point. Get comfortable with it before test day so it feels automatic during the exam.

Key Things to Remember on Test Day

• Desmos is only available on the calculator section of the SAT — not the no-calculator section.

• You can type equations in any form (slope-intercept, standard, etc.) — Desmos handles all of them.

• Zoom out if you can't see the intersection point — use the minus button or scroll out.

• The coordinates shown are exact — if Desmos shows (3, 7), that's your final answer. No rounding needed.
