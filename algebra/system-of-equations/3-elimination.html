Solving by Elimination

Elimination is often the fastest method on the SAT. Instead of isolating a variable, you add or subtract the equations to make one variable disappear entirely.

When to Use Elimination

Best for: Systems where both equations are in Ax + By = C form, especially when coefficients already match or are easy to match. Also great when the SAT asks for a combination like x + y (see Lesson 4!).

The 4 Steps
1
Choose a variable to eliminate. Pick the variable whose coefficients are easiest to make opposites.
2
Multiply one or both equations so the chosen variable's coefficients are opposites (e.g., +3y and −3y).
3
Add the equations. One variable cancels out, leaving one equation with one variable.
4
Solve, then back-substitute to find the other variable.
Worked Example 1 — Coefficients Already Match
Example 1
2x + 3y = 12
4x − 3y = 6
1
The y-coefficients are already opposites: +3y and −3y. No multiplication needed!
2
Add the equations: (2x + 3y) + (4x − 3y) = 12 + 6 → 6x = 18
3
Solve: x = 3
4
Back-substitute into equation 1: 2(3) + 3y = 12 → 3y = 6 → y = 2
✓
Answer: (x, y) = (3, 2)
Worked Example 2 — Must Multiply First
Example 2
3x + 2y = 16
x + y = 7
1
Eliminate y. Multiply equation 2 by −2: −2x − 2y = −14
2
Add to equation 1: (3x + 2y) + (−2x − 2y) = 16 + (−14) → x = 2
3
Back-substitute: 2 + y = 7 → y = 5
✓
Answer: (x, y) = (2, 5)

Pro tip: You can also eliminate by subtracting equations instead of adding — just be careful with signs. Multiplying by −1 and then adding is the safest approach to avoid sign errors.
